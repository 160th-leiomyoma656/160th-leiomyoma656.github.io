## 🛠 Features

The application provides tools for common game settings. You manage your files through a clean interface.

### Crosshair Editor
Design your crosshair visually. Adjust size, gap, thickness, and color. The preview window shows how your crosshair looks in real time. Save your designs into presets for quick switching between different styles.

### Sensitivity and eDPI Calculator
Calculate your mouse sensitivity accurately. Enter your current mouse dots per inch (DPI) and your in-game sensitivity. The app calculates your eDPI. Use the translator to sync your sensitivity requirements across multiple games.

### Key Bindings and Aliases
Assign game functions to specific keys. Map jump-throw binds, buy binds, or chat messages to your keyboard. The build tool handles the syntax required by the game engine.

### Configuration Management
The app reads your current game files. You view your active settings in a live list. Make changes and export them directly to your game folder. The tool supports multiple profiles for different practice modes or competitive play styles.

## 💡 How to use the app

1. Open the application.
2. Select your version of Counter-Strike from the main menu.
3. Click on the tab for the setting you want to change, such as Crosshair or Binds.
4. Adjust the sliders or fill in the boxes based on your preferences.
5. Click the Save button to update your autoexec.cfg file.
6. Open your game to see the changes. 

If you want to revert to your previous settings, the app maintains a backup folder. You restore your original file by selecting the Revert option in the Settings tab. 

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If the application does not detect your game folder, you set the path manually. 

1. Go to the Settings menu inside the application.
2. Select Folder Paths.
3. Click Browse and navigate to your Counter-Strike installation folder.
4. Select the folder containing your config files.
5. Save your changes.

If your settings do not apply in-game, ensure the file is named autoexec.cfg exactly. The application handles this naming during the save process, but manual renaming in the game directory can sometimes cause errors. Verify that the game is closed when you apply major changes to your configuration.
